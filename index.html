Peaks

Concept & Interface Design

"Peaks", is a social tool for mountain climbers, hikers, and trail-finders.Through this app, the user can create a profile, and set up "Goals" similar to MyFitnessPal or FitBit. These goals can be to summit a particular mountain, or complete a simple trail.

Within the app is a topographical map database where one can plan routes, find existing trails, and down- load particular segments of the map directly to their phone for long hikes without cell service.

There is the "record" feature located in the bottom right hand side of the screen, which the user can simply touch to start recording his/her path, and thus create a new trail.There is the camera button, located in the bottom center of the screen, which allows the user at any point during their hike to take a picture of their surround- ings, and information such as their coor- dinates, altitude, etc. are stored. The user can then go back later and look at their recorded route, and see pictures pop up along the topographical map where they were taken.The user can then publish their new trail, or their completion of an existing one, along with photos and notes in a sort of mini-blog format similar to twitter. These posts are kept to 140 characters or less, and can be published while on the trail of a single photo with their comment.Similar to other social sites, users can follow friends and see their posts show up on their feed, which appears on the home user screen.

Users can also follow “professional” climbers, who record and create routes consistently, just as they would a blog or Instagram feed. Through all these connections such as number of followers, recorded trails, or “saves to goals”, data is generated that can be used to create an “explore” screen (not featured) where a user can see trending routes, popular mountains, and the most liked or commented on scenery from a certain Peak.

  • Date: September 2017
  • Client: WKU IXD Project
  • Category: IX/UX Design
